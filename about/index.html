About This Site

Created to assist students in solving lent computing tasks, whilst maximising efficiency and minimising time wasted on watching unnecessary content provided by the department.

If you are interested in making this a bigger thing, e.g. make it dynamic, add in discussion board features, integrate with raven, etc., please shoot me an email at proposal@brianc.xyz

I used a relatively uncommon static site generator Hexo and one of its most popular themes, theme-NexT, to commemorate the six years (2017-22) in which I wrote blog posts about various topics, some technical, some critical and some just stupid rants about unimportant things.

In 2017. I started with writing about solutions to informatics problems and common algorithms when I still took part in informatics olympiads (all regional, never even made it to national). After I quitted around 2018, my posts shifted to a more personal style, mostly about some small projects, some interesting ideas and stupid stuff happened at school. During that time, my blog used the same framework and theme as this site does right now.

After covid, lockdown and online classes, during which I used my site as a distribution point for course-related files scattered around group chats, I started writing longer articles, some on self reflection, some on analysis of social trends, some are comments on a particular event/incident and some are a half-technical analysis of some random new software that some random company just released. During that time I also had the opportunity to use and contribute to some interesting projects include the waline comment system and onedrive-vercel-index. I am also very grateful for the many (probably?) RSS subscribers that would always leave comments on my posts no matter how stupid they were.

I’ve really spent a good chunk of my free time on writing blog posts and maintaining a blog site. During the six years of blogging, the theme of the site changed, the domain changed around multiple times, the language gradually switched from Chinese to English, and I became an early adopter of GitHub Actions using it for hexo blog deployment automation. But when sitting down in front of a markdown editor, it is still the stage, on which I present my half-true half-imaginary self, to whichever random Internet stranger that clicked into the page.

Cheers and thank you for reading all this nonsense. Oh right - I’m looking for software internship opportunities. admin@brianc.xyz
